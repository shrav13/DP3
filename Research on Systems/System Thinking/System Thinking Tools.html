System Thinking Tools

  • Brainstorming Tools : Provides a visual map of key factors involved.

    Double-Q (QQ) Diagram : AKA Fishbone or cause-and-effect diagram. Qs stand for quality and quantity. To help begin to see the whole system. Hard (quantitative) factors branch off along the top. Soft (qualitative) factors run along the bottom. Arrows leading off the major factors represent sun-factors.

  • Dynamic Thinking Tools :
    • Loops

      Reinforcing loop : Produce both growth and decay. They compound change in one direction with even more change. eg, encouragement from a supervisor is capable of producing good employee performance.

      Balancing loop : Try to bring things to a desired state and keep them there.

    Behind every growth or decay is a reinforcing loop. For every goal seeking behavior, there is a balancing loop.
    1. Behavior Over Time (BOT) Diagram : AKA reference mode diagram. Capture dynamic relationship between variables. Sketching different variables on the same graph.
    1. Causal Loop Diagrams (CLDs) : Helps in identifying reinforcing and balancing processes.

    1. System Archetypes : Helps in recognizing common system behavior patterns

  • Structural Thinking Tools : Building blocks of computer models.
    1. Graphical Function Diagram : For clarifying nonlinear relationships of one variable with other. For quantifying the effects of variables that are difficult to measure.
    1. Structure-Behavior pairs : Link a specific structure with its corresponding behavior. (exponential growth, delays, smooths, oscillations, etc.)
    1. Policy Structure Diagrams : A conceptual map of decision making process embedded in the organization. Represents the processes that drive policies.

  • Computer based Tools :
    1. Computer Model : translates all relationships identified as relevant into mathematical equations.
    1. Management Flight Stimulator : Users can recognize long term consequences of decisions by formulating strategies and making decisions based on those strategies.
    1. Learning Laboratory : Experimentation and practice field.
